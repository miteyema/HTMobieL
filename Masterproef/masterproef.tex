\begin{abstract} % Sander
Developers of mobile applications are confronted with a variety of mobile operating systems that are present on smartphones and tablets.
This is because an application has to be programmed in a language specific for the operating system.
A solution is the development of mobile web applications using HTML5.
To speed up development,  frameworks are presented that help with the functionality of the application and elements of the user interface.

Because of the variety of mobile HTML5 frameworks,  a thorough comparative study is necessary.
This thesis compares \st{}, \kendo{}, \jqm{} and \lungo{} based on five comparison criteria.
The first criterion handles popularity were the activity of frameworks on social networks is aggregated.
Secondly,  productivity is measured by measuring how long it takes to get acquainted with the framework.
The third criterion studies the usability of the framework by looking which elements or plugins are offered by the framework.
The fourth criterion looks at the support by testing the framework on eight devices.
The last criterion is performance and will look at download time and user experience.

The comparison shows that \jqm{} is the best framework.
Then, \kendo{},  \lungo{} and \st{} follow on respectively second, third and forth place.
\jqm{} has a high productivity as major advantage because on the one hand it is well-documented and on the other hand it does not impose an architecture.
The latter,  however,  is a disadvantage when looking at the usability.
\kendo{} has the MVVM architecture as most important advantage.
However,  it scores below average on performance.   % because it crashes on iOS when loading long lists.
\lungo{} only achieved a maximum score for performance because the framework is optimised for mobile usage.
\st{} is the least productive and performant in comparison with other frameworks.
By enforcing an architecture,  it scores almost as good as \kendo{} regarding usability.
All frameworks provide good support on the evaluated mobile devices.

\end{abstract}
